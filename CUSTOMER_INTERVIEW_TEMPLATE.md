# Enterprise Customer Validation Interview Template

## Objective
Validate Taskflow's value proposition with potential enterprise customers to ensure product-market fit and refine positioning before full market launch.

## Pre-Interview Preparation

### Research
- Company background and industry
- Current task management tools used
- Team size and structure
- Known productivity challenges

### Goals
- Validate core value proposition
- Identify pain points Taskflow addresses
- Understand buying process and decision criteria
- Gather feedback on pricing and features
- Assess willingness to pay

## Interview Structure (30-45 minutes)

### Opening (5 minutes)
1. Introductions and agenda
2. Confidentiality agreement
3. Warm-up: "Tell me about your role and team"

### Current State Assessment (10 minutes)
1. "How does your team currently manage tasks and projects?"
2. "What tools do you use for task management?"
3. "What are the biggest challenges with your current approach?"
4. "How do you measure team productivity?"
5. "What happens when deadlines are missed?"

### Pain Point Exploration (10 minutes)
1. "Describe a recent situation where task management caused problems"
2. "How much time do you spend coordinating tasks vs. executing?"
3. "How do you prioritize competing demands?"
4. "What would be the impact of improving task completion by 20%?"
5. "What features are missing from your current tools?"

### Solution Validation (15 minutes)
1. "How would predictive task prioritization help your team?"
2. "What would meaningful productivity metrics look like for your organization?"
3. "How important is security and compliance for your team?"
4. "What would convince you to switch from your current solution?"
5. "How do you evaluate new productivity tools?"
6. "Who else in your organization would need to approve this?"

### Pricing & Purchase Process (5 minutes)
1. "What's your typical budget for productivity tools?"
2. "How do you make purchasing decisions for team tools?"
3. "Who would be involved in evaluating Taskflow?"
4. "What would be your timeline for implementation?"

## Key Questions to Validate

### Value Proposition
- [ ] Do they recognize the problem Taskflow solves?
- [ ] Would they pay for the solution?
- [ ] Is the price point acceptable?
- [ ] Is the implementation complexity manageable?

### Features
- [ ] Is AI-powered prioritization valuable?
- [ ] Are performance metrics meaningful?
- [ ] Is enterprise security important?
- [ ] Do they need team collaboration features?

### Positioning
- [ ] Does "Work Simplified" resonate?
- [ ] Is the professional aesthetic appropriate?
- [ ] Do they prefer productivity intelligence over gamification?
- [ ] Is the differentiation from competitors clear?

## Success Indicators

### Strong Interest
- Verbal commitment to try Taskflow
- Specific use cases identified
- Timeline for evaluation mentioned
- Willingness to provide referrals

### Moderate Interest
- Acknowledges value but needs more information
- Wants to see competitor comparison
- Requests custom demo
- Asks about specific features

### Low Interest
- Says it's not a priority
- Mentions budget constraints
- Prefers current solution
- Doesn't see the value

## Follow-up Actions

### Immediate (Within 24 hours)
- Send thank you note
- Share promised resources
- Schedule follow-up if requested

### Short-term (Within 1 week)
- Provide custom demo if requested
- Send competitive analysis if needed
- Connect with technical team if required

### Medium-term (Within 1 month)
- Schedule product trial
- Provide pricing proposal
- Arrange reference customer call
